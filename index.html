1.     News Mapping:

a)     If the news has all the data with complete content, then only we will tag it with the subcategory.

b)     If we receive complete news from any source & its title is wrong or incomplete (For e.g. the title will be having just some special character or any number which is not relevant to the news), then we need to update the Title available in the news & confirm the news accordingly. (Most of the incomplete or incorrect title are fetched through Press release source.)

2.     News Junking:

a)     Native language news will be junked.

b)     Incomplete news comes in Key events will be junked.

c)     If we receive news through SEC source with sub-category then we need to check the duplicate news & compare, if the news is already auto-confirmed is having complete content then we will junk the news which is fetched from the SEC source. If the Auto-confirmed news is incomplete & the duplicate news from SEC source is complete, then we need to confirm SEC news & junk the Auto-confirmed news.

d)     News containing below keywords in Title should be junked.

                                 i.            Director/PDMR Shareholding

                               ii.            Net Asset Value or NAV

                             iii.            Form 8.3

                             iv.            Form 8.5

                               v.            Total Voting Rights

                             vi.            Notification of Major Holdings

Introduction-

This methodology documents outline how the key events categories will be assign to respective key news.

main categories.

 

Subcategory:

A subcategory is a more specific classification or subdivision within a broader main category. It provides further granularity and detail by categorizing related news. We have total 54 subcategories.
